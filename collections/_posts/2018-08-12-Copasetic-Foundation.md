>‘The remarkable high point of the evening is when Josephson has the lights lowered for a spotlight on Billie Holiday’s (Vimala Rowe) face as she sings the moving protest song Strange Fruit’- British Theatre Guide

>‘The stand out has to be Vimala who’s Holiday evoked the strong yet fragile legends essence’ - Official London Theatre

>‘But there is one song, one moment that tears at the heartstrings and makes you realise why this show was so important. ‘Strange Fruit’ sung in the show by the incomparable and spectacularly talented Rowe, catches the attention of the whole room and leaves us in awe when the lights go down, and the show ends’ - A Younger Theatre

>‘The power of some of the songs, most notably Rowe’s rendition of Holiday’s Strange Fruit’ - Musical Theatre Review
